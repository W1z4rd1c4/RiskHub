# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want RiskHub to be a respectful, technically serious project. Contributors, maintainers, and community members are expected to keep discussion constructive, specific, and professional.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a healthy community:

- giving direct, evidence-based technical feedback
- assuming good intent while challenging weak assumptions
- sharing context, repro steps, and verification details
- respecting different levels of experience and domain knowledge

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- harassment, intimidation, or discriminatory language
- personal attacks, insults, or repeated bad-faith arguments
- publishing private information without explicit permission
- disrupting review, issue, or discussion threads after a maintainer decision is clearly stated

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code of conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, and pull requests that violate these standards.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in repository spaces and in project-related public or private communication.

## Enforcement

Report unacceptable behavior to the maintainer through GitHub. For security-sensitive reports, use the process in [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md) instead of a public issue.

## Consequences

Maintainers may take any action they judge appropriate, including warning the contributor, locking conversations, closing issues or pull requests, or banning participation.
